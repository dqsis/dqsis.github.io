Before you start

Shoot first, decide later

If you're unsure whether to take it, take it. Hesitation costs shots. Editing is for later.

One photo per day — but shoot freely

The challenge is choosing one, not taking one. Shoot what you want, pick the best at the end of the day.

Phone is enough

Proximity and timing beat equipment every time. Resist the urge to apologise for the camera.

No filters

Especially on travel shots. You want to remember what it actually looked like, not the version you performed.

If you miss a day, don't catch up

One missed day is fine. Doubling up the next day is how challenges die. Move forward.

The prompt is a direction, not a rule

It loosens your eye. It doesn't own the day. If something better appears, shoot that.

Keep your phone accessible

The shot you miss is usually the one you saw but didn't reach for the phone in time.

Don't wake the family for a shot

If the light requires an alarm and a 20-minute walk, that's your call. Just don't make it everyone's.
