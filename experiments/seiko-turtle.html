Seiko Prospex

The Turtle

Few watches have earned a nickname as affectionately as the Seiko Turtle. Nearly five decades of cushion-cased, water-resistant history — from the ocean floor to the wrist of everyday enthusiasts.

A History

1965

62MAS · The Beginning

Seiko launches its first professional diver, the 62MAS, rated to 150m. It sets the foundation for all Seiko dive watches to come — a commitment to tool-watch precision at an accessible price.

1968

6159-7001 · 600m Professional

Seiko pushes into professional territory with a 600m-rated diver used on the Kon-Tiki Trans-Pacific expedition. A landmark for the brand's engineering credibility.

1976

6309-7040 · The Original Turtle

The watch that started it all. The 6309 introduces the distinctive cushion-shaped case — wider at the lugs, rounder at the sides — that earns it the "Turtle" nickname among collectors. Water resistant to 150m, powered by the reliable 6309 movement. It becomes one of the most beloved Seiko divers ever made.

1982

6309-729x · Variants

New dial variants of the 6309 appear, including the iconic black dial / golden indices combination. The Turtle's cult following grows quietly through the 1980s.

1988

7002-7000 · The Successor

The 7002 takes over from the 6309, retaining the cushion case and 150m rating. Casually referred to as the "Baby Turtle" by some collectors, it carries the lineage into the 1990s.

1996

Discontinuation

Seiko quietly discontinues the cushion-case diver line. The original Turtles move to the secondhand market, where they gain a devoted following among collectors who prize their robust simplicity.

2016

SRP777 / SRP779 · The Return

After 20 years, Seiko brings back the Turtle under the Prospex banner. The modern reissue is faithful to the original silhouette — same cushion case, same spirit — but updated with the automatic 4R36 movement (hand-winding and hacking), 200m water resistance, and Lumibrite lume. The watch community erupts with enthusiasm. Multiple colorways follow rapidly.

2018

SRPC Series · Expanding the Range

Seiko expands the modern Turtle lineup with new dial colors and the SRPC series. Special editions and limited runs make the Turtle one of the most active references in the Prospex collection.

2020

SRPE Series · New Generation

The SRPE generation introduces refreshed colorways and updated finishing. The cushion case remains unchanged — a deliberate nod to the 1976 original. New dial textures and indices variations keep the lineup fresh for a new decade of enthusiasts.

My Watch

SRPE93K1 · Prospex Turtle

The SRPE93K1 — a deep, commanding black dial with applied indices and Lumibrite fill, housed in the unmistakable cushion case. Powered by the 4R36 automatic movement with 200m water resistance. A direct descendant of the 1976 original, wearing its heritage without making a fuss about it.
