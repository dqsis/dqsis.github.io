## About me

I have spent the past fifteen years at the intersection of healthcare and technology, leading teams and building software for clinical personnel and patients. I care about how teams work and how software gets built.

### Studies 

I studied [Mechanical Engineering](http://www.mech.ntua.gr/en) at the National Technical University of Athens. At 23, my interest in mathematics and [programming](http://web.stanford.edu/class/me200c/tutorial_77/), and my curiosity to live abroad,  brought me to Graz, Austria, where I earned a PhD in Biomechanics researching [angioplasty and stents](https://diglib.tugraz.at/biomechanical-and-computational-modeling-of-atherosclerotic-arteries-2008-4).

### Work

In 2008 I came to the west coast of Sweden and worked on designing [rocket](https://www.arianespace.com/vehicle/ariane-6/) and [jet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pratt_%26_Whitney_PW1000G) engines to withstand thermo-mechanical fatigue. Working in aerospace was where I truly understood what [engineering discipline really demands](https://spacecraft.ssl.umd.edu/akins_laws.html): that the best design is the one that meets the requirements, not the one that exceeds them; that schedules only move in one direction; that the most critical work happens at the interfaces between teams and systems; and that in some fields, there is no partial credit because *most* of the analysis was right.

Between 2013 and 2017 I worked as a Consultant Manager in Virtual Product Development, moving across industries, ranging from automotive to packaging. The role was split between sales, convincing customers that my team had what it took, and hands-on engineering across a wide range of components. I learned to earn trust quickly, and to deliver on-time and budget even in unfamiliar environments. 

In January 2017 I joined Mentice, where I led a team of Project Managers building  [Virtual Reality simulation software](http://www.mentice.com/) for endovascular intervention and minimally invasive surgery. It was, in many ways, a return to biomechanics, to stenting, to the clinical world I had studied in Graz. I drove my own projects before earning the mandate to build and lead the PMO from scratch. Working directly with Abbott, Stryker, and Medtronic, I learned what it means to deliver software at the standards that the medical device industry demands.

From August 2022 to January 2024 I worked as an Innovation Manager at the [Region of Västra Götaland](https://www.vgregion.se/ov/innovationsplattformen/) and at [Sahlgrenska University Hospital](https://www.sahlgrenska.se/en/). My primary focus was bridging the public healthcare sector and private industry. For the first time I was fully on the other side, inside the clinical environment, seeing firsthand the challenges faced by physicians and their patients. Coaching clinical teams as they turned their ideas into real solutions was some of the most rewarding work I have done.

In 2024, I served as Product Owner at [Glooko](https://glooko.com/) leading the Cloud Integrations team on a platform that unified data from leading diabetes devices to support clinical and personal decision-making. Glooko was well-run in ways that are rarer than they should be. Genuine Agile discipline, a release cadence they owned, and technology that was really modern. The PO role turned out to suit me well: it sits at the intersection of technical depth, organizational clarity, and communication which is, as I have come to realize, where I do some of my best work.

In 2025 I joined [Omda](https://omda.com/) as Development Manager in the Woman & Child Business Area, leading development of Electronic Health Record systems used daily by clinical personnel across Norway, Sweden, and Finland. There is something grounding about building software that midwives and doctors depend on every single day, supporting care from the first ultrasound through early childhood.

For the full picture, find me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kiousis/).
